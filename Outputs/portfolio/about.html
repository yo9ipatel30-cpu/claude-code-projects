Bio

I'm a first-year student at the University of Virginia pursuing Neuroscience and Religious Studies, with the long-term goal of becoming a physician-scientist. My scientific work spans two UVA School of Medicine labs: in the Kapur Lab I investigate the molecular mechanisms underlying seizure pathophysiology, and in the Khetarpal Lab I study how cardiac metabolism and dyslipidemia drive cardiovascular dysfunction. Before UVA I completed independent and collaborative research at Florida Institute of Technology, where my work on physiological stress signals is being prepared for peer-reviewed publication.


Science and service are not separate pursuits for me — they're intertwined. As President of the BAPS Campus Fellowship and Director of Volunteering for Neurotech at UVA, I work to make neuroscience education and healthcare accessible to underserved communities. I'm also a published author: I've written and co-authored books on bioethics and medicine for both general and community audiences. I believe that being a good physician means understanding not just the biology of disease, but the human and ethical dimensions of care.
